# Institutional Theme Research: AI Infrastructure

## A. Cover
- Theme: AI Infrastructure
- Date: 2026-02-07
- One-sentence call: Early-cycle infrastructure capex remains durable, but value capture is narrowing to execution leaders.

## B. Exec Summary
- Structural shift: AI demand is transitioning from experimentation to production deployment with hard latency and reliability requirements.
- Why now: Capacity additions and network bottlenecks are converging with tighter return-on-capital scrutiny.
- Winners / Losers: Leaders with margin discipline and supply-chain leverage benefit; capital-intensive laggards with weak conversion de-rate.
- Valuation / market expectations: Consensus still prices broad linear demand growth; our view expects selective margin and multiple compression.
- Recommended exposure framework: Core in durable cash generators, satellite in bottleneck suppliers, optionality in event-driven asymmetry.

## C. The Story
Three years ago, AI infrastructure was priced as unconstrained demand plus unlimited incremental capital. That regime changed as deployment shifted from lab workloads to production-grade inference with strict cost, latency, and uptime targets. The constraint has moved from raw demand to economically viable scaling.

The inflection is visible in operating behavior: buyers now optimize total cost-of-ownership, vendors are judged on throughput per watt and software efficiency, and execution risk has become more important than top-line momentum. In prior cycles, aggregate demand masked poor capital discipline. In this cycle, dispersion in margin durability and cash conversion is determining relative returns.

Consensus still leans on a broad beta narrative, assuming all layers capture similar economics. Our differentiated view is that value capture is concentrating in a subset of the chain with pricing power, high switching costs, and constrained supply. That means portfolio construction should favor selective exposure rather than broad thematic baskets.

Why now is practical, not rhetorical: each quarter adds data that can falsify or confirm this concentration thesis. If guidance quality, margin conversion, and catalyst follow-through stay aligned, the thesis compounds. If not, risk must be cut quickly.

## D. Mechanics of the Theme
- Value chain: compute silicon, memory, networking, cloud capacity, and software optimization layers compete for economic rents.
- Operating leverage vs capital intensity: durable winners combine gross-margin resilience with disciplined capex and R&D payback.
- Macro sensitivity: rates affect duration, labor tightness affects delivery timelines, and policy/export rules reshape supply routing.

## F. Winners, Losers, and Second-Order Effects
- Direct beneficiaries: bottleneck suppliers with pricing power and high utilization visibility.
- Indirect beneficiaries: power/cooling optimization, network orchestration, and tooling layers.
- Disrupted incumbents: low-differentiation providers with rising capital intensity and weak conversion.
- Second-order impacts: enterprise IT budget reallocation, regional capacity arbitrage, and revised hurdle rates.

## G. Capital Allocation Playbook
- Public equities (core): concentrate in names with proven margin durability and revision quality.
- Public equities (satellite): event-driven opportunities with defined catalyst windows.
- Private markets implications: avoid growth-only underwriting without clear unit-economics pathways.
- Infrastructure / picks-and-shovels: prioritize constrained assets with pricing leverage.
- Optionality trades: use bounded-risk structures around catalyst clusters.
- What not to own: high-duration stories with weak free-cash conversion and policy fragility.

## H. Risks, Falsifiers, and Monitoring Dashboard
1. Falsifier: margin conversion deteriorates for two consecutive reporting periods in the leadership cohort.
2. Falsifier: revision breadth collapses while consensus expectations remain elevated.
3. Falsifier: policy shocks remove key demand channels without replacement.
4. Monitoring indicators: revision breadth, margin bridges, capex efficiency, and scenario repricing cadence.
5. Risk protocol: cut risk when two or more falsifiers trigger simultaneously.
